My knowledge

I, Ben Michaelis, have learned a great deal of things for fun, and I consider myself a particularly curious person. Often determined to perfect something, or make something for my peace of mind, I will spend hours on hours working on a project. It's all too easy for me to disagree slightly with the color of an element, and slip into a period of constantly tweaking something until it's perfect. I'll go to great lengths to change one little thing, with only the promise that I'll like it more once I'm done. These perfectionistic tendencies coupled with my creativity, and free time have led me down many deep rabbit holes, many of which were fueled only by one simple question. But enough about how I learn, what about what I know?

I played with boolean algebra. Bored by calculus, and to some degree running from it, I learned all I could about how to make equations, logic circuits, and truth tables, all for the sake of simulating the mechanisms of a computer in a game.

I learned about computer architecture. Figured out how the CPU does math, stores memory, and counts instructions, all because I wanted to make my own computer in a game like everyone else.

I learned programming concepts and applications while trying to make a mod for a game. The primary language was Lua, but I also learn a bit about C++ and about data structures like an array, stack, or linked-list.

I didn't understand how classes worked in programs so I spent several day experimenting and researching the ins and outs of their functionality.

I wanted to learn how to use a card design program to make my own card game, forcing me to learn many things along the way. I learned how spreadsheets could be accessed by a program. I learned many important elements of game design. And I learned how to create a game manual by making one.

I saw some cool spreadsheet tips on youtube shorts, so I spent a few days learning about how to program things in a spreadsheet. I figured out how to make variables, tables, and expressions to process large amounts of data quickly, and display the results in all sorts of ways.

Aside from all my learning in my free time, I also learned much from school. Whenever I could, I tried to understand the best way to solve problems, create plans, and search for answers. My objective in school was not just to remember the info for the next test, but to really understand it, so that I could apply it. My curriculum was pretty standard, so I tried to make the most of it, not to stand out, but because it was boring otherwise.
